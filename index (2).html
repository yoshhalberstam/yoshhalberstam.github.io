About

I'm an economist and data scientist who builds causal measurement systems to understand what drives behavior, adoption, and outcomes at scale.

I design and lead applied research across large observational datasets and real-world systems—studying how information spreads through networks, how incentives and pricing structures shape behavior, and how institutional design affects distributions and growth. Published in AER: Insights, AEJ: Applied, Journal of Public Economics. Covered by The New York Times.

My edge isn't just causal methods—it's product thinking with rigor. I specialize in framing the right questions, designing credible measurement in noisy environments, and translating analysis into decisions that engineering, product, and leadership teams can act on.

I'm currently focused on applying these methods to AI systems and experimentation infrastructure, and understanding how incentives shape deployment and real-world impact of machine learning and platform technologies.
